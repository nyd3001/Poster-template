\textbf{Motivation.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Existing audio benchmarks fail to assess fine-grained understanding.
    \item Annotations are often coarse, generic, or single-perspective.
    \item Evaluation metrics penalize novel details while tolerating overly safe descriptions.
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.25cm}
\textbf{Solution.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item MECAT builds a multi-expert pipeline for detailed audio understanding.
    \item LLM-CoT synthesizes expert outputs into dense captions and QA pairs.
    \item DATE rewards specific details and penalizes generic terms.
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.25cm}
\textbf{Key advantages.}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Scale}: \PosterHighlight{20K} clips from ACAV100M across extended audio domains.
    \item \textbf{Density (Per Clip)}:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Caption: \PosterHighlight{18} refs spanning systemic, content-related/unrelated aspects.
        \item QA: \PosterHighlight{5} pairs probing perception, analysis, and reasoning capabilities.
    \end{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Discrimination}: DATE robustly separates accurate details from vague errors.
\end{itemize}
